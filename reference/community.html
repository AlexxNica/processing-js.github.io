Community

IRC Channel

The Processing.js IRC channel has moved to irc.mozilla.org where the PJS developers are hanging out. Need information, have feature suggestions, bugs? Join irc://irc.mozilla.org/processing.js and we will help you get the most out of Processing.js.

Bug Tracking with LightHouse

The development team have chosen to go with LightHouse as the bug tracking system for Processing.js. If you think you may have stumbled accross a bug, or you are interested in joining in the development, visit Processing.js at Lighthouse.

GitHub Repository

The current fork of the Processing.js repo at GitHub. Developers wishing to contribute to Processing.js should sign-up at GitHub and pull the code to their local machines. Then any changes you make can be easily integrated into the master version.

Open Source @ Seneca

David Humphy from Open Source @ Seneca has put together an excellent team of students, who are working on Processing.js as part of Mozilla's Processing for The Open Web initiative. Among the students are members of Cathy Leung's C3DL team, who developed the C3DL JavaScript Library for use with The Canvas3D plugin for Firefox. Using this technology they had created, they developed a commercial web-solution, Motion View, to assist in the tedious clean-up-operation after a busy day's 3D motion capture. Motion View is now being used by companies such as Bedlam Games, who are working the very-cool... Scratch The Ultimate DJ.

Processing For The Open Web

Processing for the Web is a Mozilla initiative to bring a complete implementation of the Processing language, (including 3D hardware support), to the open web. Read more.

Google Group

A very helpful group of Processing.js enthusiasts who frequently share code, techniques and collaborate on new ideas. A great place for beginners to get started.
